What is Tet holiday?


History of Tet

The Lunar New Year holiday was originally introduced to Vietnam by the Chinese. The historic period of Chinese rule played a crucial role in shaping Vietnamese traditions, including the celebration of Tết.

The name "Tết"

The name "Tết" is a shortening of "Tết Nguyên Đán", literally written as "Tết" which means festivals(only used in festival names) and "nguyên đán" which means the first day of the year

When and how long?

Tết is based on the Vietnamese lunar calendar and typically falls between late January and early February in the Gregorian calendar. Tết is a national public holiday in Vietnam and usually lasts 5-7 days.

Why is Tet important?

Tết is deeply rooted in Vietnamese history and tradition. It reflects the country's rich cultural heritage, customs, and values. It marks the arrival of spring, symbolizing renewal, family reunions, and hopes for a prosperous year ahead.
